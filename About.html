From Tucson to Gaza — A Shared Struggle

When you can’t get credit, can’t buy a house, and can’t rely on the system — you learn to survive differently. Buying a 1999 trailer and rebuilding it with reclaimed materials wasn’t just about saving money — it was a statement about self-reliance, creativity, and dignity.

The Gaza Rebuild Plan takes that mindset global. Imagine teaching families to rebuild homes using recycled concrete, melted-down metals, and solar-powered tools made from scrap electronics. Imagine turning rubble into raw material — transforming broken neighborhoods into living, breathing art installations of resilience.

This repository doesn’t claim to have the full plan — it’s a starting point. It’s open-source so builders, designers, engineers, and everyday people can share, adapt, and grow these concepts.
