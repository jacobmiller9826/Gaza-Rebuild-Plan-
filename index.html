Building Hope From What’s Left

This project started as a personal story — living in Tucson, Arizona without credit, I bought a vintage 1999 Aljo Ally trailer and rebuilt it using reclaimed wood, metal, and furniture pulled from local yards, alleys, and construction scraps. I rented a lot in a small trailer park for $500 a month — that was my foundation. From there, I learned how broken things can be reborn.

Now, that same concept is the seed for a larger idea: helping rebuild Gaza — or any community destroyed by conflict or poverty — using what’s available. Not through big corporations or government loans, but through resourceful, decentralized rebuilding. This repository is the first draft of that idea.
